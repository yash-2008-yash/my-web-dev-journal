## The Journey

**This is it.** Chapter 139 marks the ending of the **Sigma Web Dev Course** inside my web dev journal.

I started with the basics, HTML tags, CSS boxes that wouldn't center, and `console.log()` which felt like magic at that moment.

Now... I have the knowledge of Javascript fundamentals, Node.js & Express.js, MongoDB, React, Next.js, and etc. It's been such a long and beautiful journey.

There were moments like *"Why the fuck isn't this `<div>` getting centered?"*. Now, here I am deploying full-stack apps. I know it's not a big deal, but a deal is a deal, man! I have come so far. That's what matters to me.

## So... What's next?

There's no such thing as a **'full stop'** in programming journey.  
It's a never-ending story.

I'm going to keep learning new things everyday.  
Build projects based on what I've learned so far, because theory alone will only take you so far. Practical knowledge pushes you to "do" things, not just "see" others do the work.

Also, one advice from me:

Don't just keep your work in GitHub and hope people will see it.  
Remember: ***"Closed mouths don't get fed."***  

You have to make your work public and share it with everyone. Even if it's a simple app, share it. It doesn't matter. Every developer started off with simple projects. Don't be ashamed of your work. Share it, no matter how small it is.

Keep going! Build some stupid projects, break things, learn to fix it, but never stop!  
Maintain consistency, and you'll become an unrecognizable version of yourself.
