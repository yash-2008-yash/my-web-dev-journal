# VERCEL

This is one of the best things for you. Like **literally!**  
You make an app *(small or big, it doesn't matter)*, and you want the world to see it.  
If you want that, you can share the link to it. Simple as that.

Consider you made a portfolio website, and you want your friends to see it.  
You can send the link `http://localhost:3000` to your friend, and they will see it.  
If you thought this was true, then consider yourself a fool.

It's `localhost` for a reason. It runs 'locally' on your computer. Only you can see it. If you send the localhost link to someone else, they won't see shit. That's because their computer doesn't have your app files. **You do.**

That's why you should host your project into the internet. Now, that link, you can share it with anybody. That's hosting. Now you'll be like *"Bro, I don't have money for buying domain to publish my app."* Don't worry. I got you covered. That's what this chapter is about.

---

Vercel is a cloud platform which can be used to deploy your apps into the internet... **for free!**  
Yes, it's absolutely free. But, you gotta know that 'free' means you'll have some limitations.

But that's more than enough for our simple personal projects.

You can deploy it for free and get a free domain *(branded, obviously)*. You can share that link with anyone, and they can see your app. For example, if you create an app called "Bullshit", you'll get a domain something like `bullshit.vercel.app`. If you want your own domain like `bullshit.com`, `bullshit.in`, etc. then you gotta pay for it.

You can buy a domain from any service and link it to Vercel DNS.

### All right. All right. All right. Lemme show how you can deploy your app.

<br>

### `Step 1` : Create an app first.

This is the most important step, obviously.  
I mean, if you don't have an app ready, what are you gonna publish, man?

<br>

### `Step 2` : Create a Vercel account.

Choose **Hobby Plan**. It's the free plan, just with a fancy name.

<br>

### `Step 3` : Push your app to GitHub.

Create a repository dedicated to your app.

<br>

### `Step 4` : Import that repository in Vercel.

You will see your GitHub repos in Vercel. Import your app's repo and answer some basic questions, and that's it. Vercel will start deploying your app.

After deployment, your app's live! You can share that link and show off your app.
